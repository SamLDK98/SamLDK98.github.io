Linear Algebra

New Notation

Column vectors are written vertically using matrix notation rather than coordinate notation.

For example, instead of $(6,2)$, we write:

$$ \begin{bmatrix} 6\\ 2 \end{bmatrix} $$

Vector Addition

Simply add corresponding components:

$$ \begin{bmatrix} 1\\ 2\\ 1 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 3\\ -1\\ 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4\\ 1\\ 5 \end{bmatrix} $$

Scalar multiplication

Multiply each component by the scalar:

$$ 2 \begin{bmatrix} 1\\ 2\\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2\\ 4\\ 2 \end{bmatrix} $$

Linear combinations

Scale each vector and then add the corresponding components.:

$$ 2 \begin{bmatrix} 1\\ 2\\ 1 \end{bmatrix} + 3 \begin{bmatrix} 1\\ -1\\ 2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 5\\ 1\\ 8 \end{bmatrix} $$

Expressing a system of linear equations

Imagine:

$$\begin{cases} x + y = 2\\2x - y = 1\\x+2y=5 \end{cases}$$

We express this as:

$$ x\begin{bmatrix} 1\\ 2\\ 1 \end{bmatrix} + y\begin{bmatrix} 1\\ -1\\ 2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2\\ 1\\ 5 \end{bmatrix} $$

Or alternatively:

$$ \begin{bmatrix} 1&1\\ 2&-1\\ 1&2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x\\ y \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2\\ 1\\ 5 \end{bmatrix} $$

Shorthand

$$ A\mathbf{x}=\mathbf{b} $$

This equation describes a linear transformation taking an input vector $\mathbf{x}$ and mapping it to an output vector $\mathbf{b}$.

$A=$ matrix representing the transformation

$\mathbf{x}=$ input vector

$\mathbf{b}=$ output vector

$$A=\begin{bmatrix} 1&1\\2&-1\\1&2\end{bmatrix}$$

$$ \mathbf{x}= \begin{bmatrix} x\\ y \end{bmatrix} $$

$$\mathbf{b}= \begin{bmatrix} 2\\ 1\\ 5 \end{bmatrix}$$
