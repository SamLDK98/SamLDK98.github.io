Every frame is a distinct microstate. The device reads one number from it — the count of dots on the left — and maps that to one of nine colours. Different microstates produce the same colour: that mapping is the coarse-graining. Let it run, and the middle colours fill faster than the extremes — not because the dots prefer them, but because far more microstates land there.
