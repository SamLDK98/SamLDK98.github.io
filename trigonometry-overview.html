Trigonometry

Overview

Opening

Trigonometry translates to triangle measurement.

In fact, we are most often more interested in angles.

It just happens that connecting the lines that possess an angle to create a triangle is the simplest method of measuring angles.

Additionally, trigonometry revolves more so around circles. Angles are most easily understood as fractions of a circle.

Typically, I like to move through these explanations conceptually, often with a focus on the historical development of ideas. However, in this case, I think it would be more productive to jump straight into learning a few things without much context. We will then go back and understand things more conceptually afterwards.

Trigonometric Functions

Initially, the sides of triangles were understood in terms of ratios, rather than functions.

Given 3 different sides, there are 6 possible ratios. We gave names to these ratios: sine, cosine, tangent, cotangent, secant, and cosecant.

$$\sin(\theta) = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{hypotenuse}} \qquad \cos(\theta) = \frac{\text{adjacent}}{\text{hypotenuse}} \qquad \tan(\theta) = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}}$$

$$ \csc(\theta) = \frac{\text{hypotenuse}}{\text{opposite}} \qquad \sec(\theta) = \frac{\text{hypotenuse}}{\text{adjacent}} \qquad \cot(\theta) = \frac{\text{adjacent}}{\text{opposite}} $$

Given a right-angled triangle and its side lengths, we can divide particular pairs of sides to produce ratios associated with the triangle's angles.
