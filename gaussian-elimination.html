Linear Algebra

Gaussian elimination

We have already seen the geometric intuition for this process; now, we will work through an abstract example. Essentially, we are recursively simplifying a constrained system by removing shared structure.

  • Choose a variable to eliminate.
  • Use one equation as the pivot row.
  • Remove that variable from the rows below.
  • Move to the next variable.
  • Repeat.

Then solve upward via back-substitution.

Initial system

$$ \begin{aligned} E_{1}:&\ 2a-3b+5c+d=-41\\ E_{2}:&\ 7a+2b-c=-28\\ E_{3}:&\ -a+2b-7c-2d=46\\ E_{4}:&\ 3a+7b-6c+d=31 \end{aligned} $$

Let $E_{1}$ be the pivot.

Let $a$ be the target variable.

Find $\operatorname{LCM}$ of coefficients of $a$ between $E_{1}$ and $E_{2}$: $14a$.

Scale both rows to the common coefficient, subtract, and replace $E_{2}$ with the result.

$$7E_{1}-2E_{2}:\ -25b+37c+7d=-231$$

Updated system

$$ \begin{aligned} E_{1}:&\ 2a-3b+5c+d=-41\\ E_{2}:&\ -25b+37c+7d=-231\\ E_{3}:&\ -a+2b-7c-2d=46\\ E_{4}:&\ 3a+7b-6c+d=31 \end{aligned} $$

Again, but with...

$E_{1}$ and $E_{3}$:

$$E_{1}:2a-3b+5c+d=-41$$

$$2E_{3}:-2a+4b-14c-4d=92$$

$$E_{1}+2E_{3}:b-9c-3d=51$$

$E_{1}$ and $E_{4}$:

$$E_{1}:2a-3b+5c+d=-41$$

$$E_{4}:3a+7b-6c+d=31$$

$$(3E_{1})-(2E_{4}):-23b+27c+d=-185$$

Updated system

$$ \begin{aligned} E_{1}:&\ 2a-3b+5c+d=-41\\ E_{2}:&\ -25b+37c+7d=-231\\ E_{3}:&\ b-9c-3d=51\\ E_{4}:&\ -23b+27c+d=-185 \end{aligned} $$

Now, let $E_{2}$ be the pivot and reduce $E_{3}$ and $E_{4}$ to two variables.

$$(E_{2}+25E_{3}):-188c-68d=1044$$

$$\phantom{(E_{2}+25E_{3}):}-47c-17d=261$$

$$(23E_{2}-25E_{4}):176c+136d=-688$$

$$\phantom{(23E_{2}-25E_{4}):}22c+17d=-86$$

Updated system

$$ \begin{aligned} E_{1}:&\ 2a-3b+5c+d=-41\\ E_{2}:&\ -25b+37c+7d=-231\\ E_{3}:&\ -47c-17d=261\\ E_{4}:&\ 22c+17d=-86 \end{aligned} $$

Only one more variable to eliminate. In this case, it's already set up nicely to remove $d$. We will use $E_{3}$ as the pivot.

$$E_{3}+E_{4}:-25c=175\rightarrow c=-7$$

Updated system

$$ \begin{aligned} E_{1}:&\ 2a-3b+5c+d=-41\\ E_{2}:&\ -25b+37c+7d=-231\\ E_{3}:&\ -47c-17d=261\\ E_{4}:&\ c=-7 \end{aligned} $$

Now begins the phase of back-substitution. Just plug in $c=-7$ to $E_{3}$, and so on...

Name Equation With known values plugged in Unknown value revealed
$E_{1}$ $2a-3b+5c+d=-41$ $2a-3(0)+5(-7)+4=-41$ $a=-5$
$E_{2}$ $-25b+37c+7d=-231$ $-25b+37(-7)+7(4)=-231$ $b=0$
$E_{3}$ $-47c-17d=261$ $-47(-7)-17d=261$ $d=4$
$E_{4}$ $c=-7$

Final solution: $a=-5$, $b=0$, $c=-7$, $d=4$.
