Trigonometry

Introduction

"Trigonometry" translates to triangle measurement.

Yet angles are the real subject of interest; constructing triangles is just an effective method for working with them.

But why are angles interesting? What even is an angle?

I would highly recommend pausing and thinking about this second question. Learning is only satisfying when driven by curiosity, so make yourself interested in this question. Interrogate what you think you know. If you can discover what you don't know, it will naturally motivate the search for clarity.

So...

Perhaps you pictured an arc between two lines.

Does the arc represent some kind of distance between the two lines?

Why is it curved rather than straight if it merely represents distance?

What's the nature of this distance? Is it spatial?

Does the angle increase as the distance between the two lines increases?

If you can't resolve all of these questions just yet, don't fret. There are many interesting ways to think about angles. One of my favourites is as fractions of circles.

We will clarify everything over the coming chapters. However, before we get stuck into the slightly flavourless meat of basic trigonometry, there is one final point I want to make.

Trigonometry leads to a fascinating revelation about the relationship between geometry, algebra, and imaginary numbers. This is captured in Euler's Formula, often cited as "the most beautiful equation in mathematics". The goal of this course is to reveal its meaning to you.
