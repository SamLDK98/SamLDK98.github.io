Trigonometry

Introduction

"Trigonometry" translates to triangle measurement.

Yet angles are the real subject of interest.

An angle is a measure of the difference between two orientations.

The orientation of an object refers to how it is rotated within a space.

It might be tempting to intuit some sense of distance or motion.

When see an angle depicted visually, it is typically a curved line. But notice that the length of this curved line is arbitrary.

It may therefore be a misleading visual aid.

It we imagine the circle partitioned as shown below, we can see more clearly what angle refers to. Notice that the curved lines all cross the same number of partitions (6), regardless of their length or even wonkiness.

Angle can be seen as a measure of how many of these lines must be crossed to rotate one orientation to the other.

We can think of each partition as a discrete orientation, and each turn to a new partition as a unit of rotation.
