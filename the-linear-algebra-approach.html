Another way to think about systems of equations

Previously, we look at an equation and imagined:

  1. A space with the number of axes as variables.
  2. Every coordinate in that space being moved, as specified by the coefficients of the variables.
  3. Every coordinate being summed and represented on a one-dimensional space, i.e. a line.
  4. The constant as a point on that line.

Now, we can do something similar when we see a system of equations.

$$\begin{cases} 4x - 2y = 2\\2x + 4y = 16 \end{cases}$$

No. We do two separate collapses onto lines (temporarily ignoring the constants).

Imagine we want to see where the point (3, 4) moves to.

So:

  • $E_{1}$ becomes 4 on the line.
  • $E_{2}$ becomes 22 on another line.

Hm. Two lines. That's two axes.

Just imagine one line rotating until perpendicular. Our points drift out to meet each other, revealing a coordinate in new 2D plane.

So we can think of our system of equations as a new space.
